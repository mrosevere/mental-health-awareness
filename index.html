Immediate Support

If you or someone else is in danger, call 999 or go to A&E now.

If you need urgent help for your mental health, get help from NHS 111 online or call 111.

Other services you can reach out to:

  • Samaritans (UK)
  • Shout Text Service
  • NHS Mental Health Services

If you are outside the UK, please contact your local emergency number.

Stress Management

Stress is a natural response, but it doesn’t have to take control. Learn how to recognize triggers, calm your mind, and restore balance in everyday life. Explore practical techniques such as breathing exercises, journaling, and gentle movement to reduce tension and build resilience for the challenges ahead.

Sleep Tips

Good sleep is the foundation of mental wellbeing. Discover simple habits to improve your sleep routine, from creating a calming bedtime environment to managing stress before bed. Restful nights help restore balance and energy, giving you the strength to face the day ahead with clarity and focus.

Mindfulness

Mindfulness helps you stay present and calm, even when life feels overwhelming. Simple practices such as breathing exercises, guided focus, and short meditations can reduce stress and improve clarity. By training your attention, you create space for calm reflection and greater emotional balance throughout your day.

Seeking Help

You don’t have to face challenges alone. Reaching out to trusted people, professionals, or support services can provide guidance and reassurance. Asking for help is a sign of strength, and connecting with others can make recovery feel more manageable and hopeful.

Frequently Asked Questions

Feeling overwhelmed is common. Start by pausing and taking slow, deep breaths. Try grounding techniques such as focusing on your senses or writing down your thoughts. If stress continues, reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or professional for support.

Create a calming bedtime routine by limiting screen time, keeping your room cool and dark, and winding down with relaxing activities like reading or gentle stretches. Consistency is key — try to go to bed and wake up at the same time each day.
